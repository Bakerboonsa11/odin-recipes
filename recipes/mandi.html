Mandi dish

Mandi is a traditional Arabian dish that captures the essence of Middle Eastern flavors and culinary heritage. It is a feast for the senses, offering a delightful combination of tender meat, fragrant rice, and a medley of aromatic spices.

At its heart, Mandi revolves around slow-cooked meat, typically lamb or chicken, prepared in a unique way. The meat is marinated with a blend of spices, including cardamom, cloves, cinnamon, black lime, and a touch of saffron. This marinade infuses the meat with a rich, smoky flavor, creating a tantalizing aroma that wafts through the air.

Ingredients

  • Meat: The most popular choice of meat for Mandi is lamb
  • Rice: Long-grain basmati rice is traditionally used for Mandi.
  • Spices and Seasonings: Mandi relies on a blend of aromatic spices
  • The meat is marinated in a mixture of spices
  • Cooking Fat: It adds richness and enhances the overall taste.
  • Garnishes: These garnishes add texture and additional layers of flavor to the dish.
